Special Session on Fairness and Trustworthiness in Intelligent Decision Support Systems

WCCI 2026 — Maastricht, The Netherlands

Scope

Intelligent Decision Support Systems (IDSS) have become essential components in modern decision-making processes, assisting humans in domains such as healthcare, finance, logistics, education, and governance. As these systems increasingly rely on artificial intelligence, machine learning, and computational intelligence, ensuring fairness, transparency, and trustworthiness has emerged as a critical research priority. Traditional approaches often emphasize accuracy and efficiency, but still ignore critical aspects related to ethical reasoning, interpretability, and the management of uncertainty, all of which are necessary to guarantee responsible and fair decision outcomes. This special session aims to provide a multidisciplinary platform for the IEEE fuzzy systems community to explore theoretical advances, methodological developments, and practical applications that address fairness and trustworthiness in intelligent decision support. In particular, fuzzy logic and related soft computing paradigms offer powerful mechanisms to support decisions that are both explainable and aligned with human reasoning. By integrating fuzzy systems with modern IDSS, AI, machine learning, and data-driven models, researchers can design hybrid approaches that enhance the interpretability and ethical accountability of automated decision-making.

  • Fairness-aware or explainable fuzzy modeling and inference mechanisms.
  • Fuzzy and hybrid approaches for explainable and trustworthy IDSS.
  • Bias detection and mitigation in fuzzy decision-making frameworks.
  • Multi-criteria and fuzzy-based methods for fair decision support.
  • Trust modeling and uncertainty quantification in human–machine collaboration.
  • Evaluation metrics for fairness, robustness, and interpretability.
  • Consensus Reaching Processes (CRPs) and conflict resolution models.
  • Linguistic, hesitant, and probabilistic fuzzy approaches.
  • Explainable consensus models.
  • Trust-aware aggregation operators and fairness-preserving fusion mechanisms.
  • Real-world applications demonstrating fairness and trust in IDSS.
